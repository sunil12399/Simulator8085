Arithmetic instructions in 8085 microprocessor

Arithmetic Instructions are the instructions which perform basic arithmetic operations such as addition, subtraction and a few more. In 8085 microprocessor, the destination operand is generally the accumulator. In 8085 microprocessor, the destination operand is generally the accumulator.

ACI: Add Immediate to Accumulator with Carry

Description: The 8-bit data(operand) and the carry flag is added to the contents of the Accumulator, and the result is stored in the accumulator.

Flags: All flags are modified to reflect the result of addition.

ADC: Add Register to Accumulator with Carry

Description: The contents of the operand(register or memory) and the carry flag is added to the contents of the Accumulator, and the result is stored in the accumulator. However the previous carry flag is reset.

Flags: All flags are modified to reflect the result of addition.

ADD: Add Register to Accumulator

Description: The contents of the operand(register or memory) is added to the contents of the Accumulator, and the result is stored in the accumulator.

Flags: All flags are modified to reflect the result of the addition.

ADI: Add Immediate to Accumulator

Description: The 8-bit data(operand) are added to the contents of the Accumulator, and the result is stored in the accumulator.

Flags: All flags are modified to reflect the results of the addition.

SBB: Subtract Source and Borrow from Accumulator

Description: The contents of the operand(register or memory) and the Borrow flag are subtracted from the contents of the Accumulator, and the results are stored in the accumulator. However the previous Borrow flag is reset.

Flags: All flags are modified to reflect the result of subtraction.

SBI: Subtract Immediate with Borrow

Description: The 8-bit data(operand) and the borrow flag are subtracted from the contents of the Accumulator, and the results are placed in the accumulator.

Flags: All flags are modified to reflect the result of addition.

SUB: Subtract Register or Memory from Accumulator

Description: The contents of the operand(register or memory) is subtracted from the contents of the Accumulator, and the result is stored in the accumulator. The contents of the source are not altered.

Flags: All flags are modified to reflect the result of the subtraction.

SUI: Subtract Immediate from Accumulator

Description: The 8-bit data(operand) are subtracted from the contents of the Accumulator, and the result is stored in the accumulator.

Flags: All flags are modified to reflect the results of the subtraction.

INR: Increment Contents of Register/Memory by 1

The contents of the designated register/memory is incremented by 1 and the results are stored in the same place. If the operand is a memory location, it is specified by the contents of the HL register pair.

Flags: S, Z, AC, PC flags are altered to reflect the results of the operation. CY is not modified.


INX: Increment Register Pair by 1

Description: The contents of the specified register pair are incremented by 1. This instruction views the contents of the two registers as a 16-bit number.

Flags: No flags are affected.

DCR: Decrement Source by 1

Description: The contents of the designated register/memory is decremented by 1 and the results are stored in the same place. If the operand is a memory location, it is specified by the contents of the HL register pair.

Flags: S, Z, AC, PC flags are altered to reflect the results of the operation. CY is not modified.


DCX: Decrement Register Pair by 1

Description: The contents of the specified register pair are decremented by 1. This instruction views the contents of the two registers as a 16-bit number.
Flags: No flags are affected.

DAA: decimal-adjust Accumulator

Description: The contents of the accumulator are changed from a binary value to two 4-bit binary-coded decimal (BCD) digits. This is the only instruction that uses the auxiliary flag (internally) to perform the binary-to-BCD conversion.

Flags: S, Z, AC, PC, CY flags are altered to reflect the results of the operation.


DAD: Add Register Pair to H and L Registers

Description: The 16-bit contents of the specified register pair are added to the contents of the HL register and the sum is asved in the HL register. The contents of the source registerpair are not altered.

Flags: If the result is larger than 16 bits the CY flag is set. No other falgs are affected.

Logical Instructions in 8085 Microprocessor

ANA: Logical AND with Accumulator

Description: The contents of the operand(register or memory) are logically ANDed with the contents of the Accumulator, and the result is stored in the accumulator.

Flags: S, Z, P are modified to reflect the result of the operation. CY is reset. In 8085, AC is set.

ANI: AND Immediate with Accumulator

Description: The 8-bit data(operand) is ANDed with the contents of the Accumulator, and the results are stored in the accumulator.
Flags: S, Z, P are modified to reflect the result of the operation. CY is reset. In 8085, AC is set.

ORA: Logically OR with Accumulator

Description: The contents of the operand(register or memory) are logically ORed with the contents of the Accumulator, and the result is stored in the accumulator.

Flags: S,Z, P are modified to reflect the result of the operation. CY and AC are reset.

ORI: Logically OR Immediate

Description: The 8-bit data(operand) and the carry flag is ANDed with the contents of the Accumulator, and the results are stored in the accumulator.
Flags: S,Z, P are modified to reflect the result of the operation. CY and AC are reset.

XRA: Exclusive OR with Accumulator

Description: The contents of the operand(register or memory) are Exclusive ORed with the contents of the Accumulator, and the result is stored in the accumulator.

Flags: S,Z, P are modified to reflect the result of the operation. CY & AC is reset.

XRI: Exclusive OR Immediate with Accumulator

Description: The 8-bit data(operand) are Exclusive ORed with the contents of the Accumulator, and the results are stored in the accumulator.
Flags: S,Z, P are modified to reflect the results of the operation. CY & AC are reset.

CMP: Compare with Accumulator

Description: The contents of the operand(register or memory) are compared with the contents of the accumulator. Both the contents are preserved and the comparison is shown by setting the flags as follows:

If(A) < (Reg/Mem): Carry flag is set and Zero flag is reset.

If(A) = (Reg/Mem): Zero flag is set and Carry flag is reset.

if(A) > (Reg/Mem): Carry and Zero flags are reset.

The comparison of two bytes is performed by subtracting the contents of the operand from the contents of the accumulator, However, neither contents are modified.

Flags: S, P, AC are also modified in addition to Z, and CY to reflect the results of the operation.

CPI: Compare Immediate with Accumulator

Description: The second byte (8-bit data) is compared with the contents of the accumulator. Both the contents are preserved and the comparison is shown by setting the flags as follows:

If(A) < (Reg/Mem): Carry flag is set and Zero flag is reset.

If(A) = (Reg/Mem): Zero flag is set and Carry flag is reset.

if(A) > (Reg/Mem): Carry and Zero flags are reset.

The comparison of two bytes is performed by subtracting the data byte from the contents of the accumulator. However, neither contents are modified.

Flags: S, P, AC are also modified in addition to Z, and CY to reflect the results of the operation.

CMA: Complement Accumulator

Description: The contents of the accumulator are complemented.

Flags: No Flags are affected.

RAL: Rotate Accumulator Left through Carry

Description: Each binary bit of the Accumulator is rotated left by one position through the carry flag. Bit D7 is placed in the Carry flag and the bit in the Carry flag is placed in the least significant position D0.

Flags: CY is modified according to bit D7. S, Z, AC, P are not affected.

RAR: Rotate Accumulator Right through Carry

Description: Each binary bit of the Accumulator is rotated right by one position through the carry flag. Bit D0 is placed in the Carry flag and the bit in the Carry flag is placed in the most significant position D7.

Flags: CY is modified according to bit D0. S, Z, AC, P are not affected.

RLC: Rotate Accumulator Left

Description: Each binary bit of the Accumulator is rotated left by one position. Bit D7 is placed in the position of D0. as well as in the Carry flag.

Flags: CY is modified according to bit D7. S, Z, AC, P are not affected.

RRC: Rotate Accumulator Right

Description: Each binary bit of the Accumulator is rotated right by one position. Bit D0 is placed in the position of D7. as well as in the Carry flag.

Flags: CY is modified according to bit D0. S, Z, AC, P are not affected.

CMC: Complement Carry

Description: The Carry flag is compllemented.

Flags: The Carry Flag is affecteed. No other Flags are affected.

STC: Set Carry

Description: The Carry flag is set to 1

Flags: No other flags are affected.

Machine Control instructions in 8085 Microprocessor

HLT: Halt and Enter Wait State

Description: The MPU finishes executting the current instruction and halts any further execution. The MPU enters the Halt Acknowledge machine cycle and Wait states are inserted inn every clock period. The address and the data bus are placed in high impedance state. The contents of the registers are unaffected during the HLT state. An interrupt or reset is necessary to exit from the Halt state.

Flags: No flags are affected.


NOP: No Operation

Description: No Operation is performed. the instruction is fetched and decoded; however no operation is executed.

Flags: No flags are affected.


DI: Disable Interrupts

Description: The Interrupt Enable flip-flop is reset and all the Interrupts except the TRAP(8085) are disabled.

Flags: No flags are affected.


EI: Enable Interrupts

Description: The Interrupt Enable flip-flop is set and all the Interrupts are enabled.

Flags: No flags are affected.

RIM: Read interrupt mask

Description: This instruction is used to read the status of interrupts 7.5, 6.5, 5.5 and read serial data input bit.

Flags: No flags are affected.

SIM: Set interrupt mask

Description: This instruction is used to implement the interrupts 7.5, 6.5, 5.5, and serial data output.

Flags: No flags are affected.

Branching instructions in 8085 Microprocessor

JMP: Jump Unconditionally

Description: The program sequence is transferred to the address specified by the 16-bit address. This is a 3-byt instruction; the second byte specifies he low-order byte and the third byte specifies the high-order byte.

Flags: No Flags are affected.

CALL: Unconditional Subroutine Call

Description: The program sequence is transferred to the address specified by the operand. Before the transfer, the address of the next instruction to CALL (the contents of the program counter) is pushed on the Stack.

Flags: No Flags are affected.

RET: Return from subroutine unconditionally

Description: The program sequence is transferred from the subroutine to the calling program.

Flags: No Flags are affected.

PCHL: Load the program counter with HL contents

Description: The contents of registers H & L are copied into the program counter. The contents of H are placed as the high-order byte and the contents of L as the low-order byte.

Flags: No flags are affected.

Data Transfer Instructions in 8085 Microprocessor

MVI: Move Immediate 8-bit

Description: The 8-bit data are stored in the destination register. If the operand is a memory location, it is specified by the contents of the HL register pair.

Flags: No flags are affected.

LXI: Load Register Pair Immediate

Description: The 16-bit data in the operand is loaded in the designated register pair.

Flags: No flags are affected.


MOV: Move- Copy from source to Destination

Description: The contents of the source register is copied into the into the destination register. If one of the operand is a memory location, it is specified by the contents of the HL register pair. The contents of source memory location are not altered.

Flags: No flags are affected.


LDA: Load Accumulator Direct

Description: The contents of a memory loacation, specified by a 16-bit address in the operand, are copied to the accumulator. The contents of the source are not altered.

Flags: No flags are affected.


LDAX: Load Accumulator Indirect

Description: The contents of a designated register pair point to a memory locaation. The instruction copies the contents of that memory location into the accumulator. The contents of neither register pair nor the memory location are not altered.

Flags: No flags are affected.

STA: Store Accumulator Direct

Description: The contents of the accumulator are copied to a memory loacation specified by the operand. The contents of the accumulator are not altered.

Flags: No flags are affected.

STAX: Store Accumulator Indirect

Description: The contents of the accumulator are copied to a memory loacation specified by the contents of the operand (register pair). The contents of the accumulator are not altered.

Flags: No flags are affected.

IN: Input Data to Accumulator from a port with 8-bit Address

Description: The contents of the input port designated in the operand are read and loaded into the accumulator.

Flags: No flags are affected.

OUT: Output Data from Accumulator to a port with 8-bit Address

Description: The contents of the accumulaator are copied into the output port designated by the operand.

Flags: No flags are affected.


LHLD: Load H and L Registers Direct

Description: The contents of the memory location pointed out by the 16-bit address is copied into the L register and copies the contents of the next memory location in register H. The contents of source memory location are not altered.

Flags: No flags are affected.


SHLD: Store H and L Registers Direct

Description: The contents of register L are stored in the memory location pointed out by the 16-bit address in the operand, and copies the contents of the H register are stored in the next memory location. The contents of HL register pair are not altered.

Flags: No flags are affected.

XTHL: Exchange H and L with top of stack

Description: The contents of the L register are exchanged with the stack location pointed out by the contents of the stack pointer register. The contents of the H register are exchanged with the next stack location (SP+1).

Flags: No flags are affected.

SPHL: Copy H and L registers to the stack pointer

Description: The instruction loads the contents of the H and L registers into the stack pointer register. The contents of the H register provide the high-order address and the contents of the L register provide the low-order address.

Flags: No flags are affected.

POP: Pop off stack to the register pair

Description: The contents of the memory location pointed out by the stack pointer register are copied to the low-order register (C, E, L, status flags) of the operand. The stack pointer is incremented by 1 and the contents of that memory location are copied to the high-order register (B, D, H, A) of the operand. The stack pointer register is again incremented by 1.

Flags: No flags are affected.

PUSH: Push the register pair onto the stack

Description: The contents of the register pair designated in the operand are copied onto the stack in the following sequence. The stack pointer register is decremented and the contents of the high order register (B, D, H, A) are copied into that location. The stack pointer register is decremented again and the contents of the low-order register (C, E, L, flags) are copied to that location

Flags: No flags are affected.

XCHG: Exchange H and L with D and E

Description: The contents of register H are exchanged with the contents of register D, and the contents of register L are exchanged with the contents of register E.

Flags: No flags are affected.
